Explore Yosemite

Hiking

Group hikes let you experience Yosemite with like-minded individuals. From a biking & hiking combo to an all-day adventure, you'll find plenty of ways to experience Yosemite with new friends!

Bike to Hike Tour

Rent a bike or bring your own and join us for the perfect way to explore the valley floor. We’ll ride our bikes to scenic locations or trailheads and then take short hikes from there. Along the way you’ll learn about Yosemite history, geology, ecology and see some of our favorite spots. Our group sizes are small so we go at your pace, and can customize the tour based on your interests. Bike rentals are available at Curry Village and Yosemite Lodge Bike Stands. The cost of bike rental is not included in the cost of the Bike to Hike Tour.

Duration: 2.5 hours
Cost: $20 per person; 10 and under $5 with paying adult. Participants need to know how to ride a bicycle.

Discovery Hikes

For people who agree that it's about the journey rather than the destination, the relaxed-paced Discover Yosemite Hikes are perfect, with stops along the way to smell the flowers, watch rock climbers, take pictures and just have fun. With our small group sizes, there is plenty of flexibility to make this Yosemite group hiking experience fit your interests. Must be a minimum age of 12.

Duration: 3.5 hours, covering roughly 3-4 miles.
Cost: $30 per person

Adventure Hike: Glacier Point to Yosemite Valley via Panorama Trail

Join the Glacier Point Tour for a one-way bus ride up to Glacier Point. Then, hike down to the Valley with one of our fun and friendly guides via the Panorama Trail. Our guides are experienced locals who are excited to share their knowldge and love of the trail with you. Small group sizes allow a personalized touch and flexible itinerary. Plan to walk roughly 8 miles during this 8 hour trip.

Duration: 8 hours , covering 6-8 miles
$75.00 per person, price includes bus ticket.

Adventure Hike

Spend a day enjoying Yosemite with one of our fun and friendly guides. Even the most popular trails are more enjoyable when you have someone who can tell you about the places that you are hiking through, (and possibly help carry some of the extra water). Our guides are experienced locals who are excited to share their knowledge and love of the park with you. Our small group sizes allow them to lead personalized, flexible group hiking excursions in Yosemite. Must be a minimum age of 12.

Durations: 6 hours, covering 6+ miles
Cost: $50 per person

Skiing

The first in the West, the Yosemite Ski and Snowboard School was established in 1928 and today employs a staff of 25 instructors, many of whom are members of the Professional Ski Instructors of America®.

Guaranteed Learn-to-Ski/Snowboard Packages

These are the perfect courses for beginner skiers and snowboarders. Learn to ride the Bruin chairlift and ski or board down on your first day, or you can keep coming back (on us) until you do. Equipment rental, beginner lift ticket, and ski lessons included.

Ski Lessons for Children

Little ones love to slide on the snow. Start your kids skiing early in the Badger Pups children's ski/snowboard lessons program, perfect for children ages 4 to 6. On weekends and holidays during the ski season, the All Day Kids Camp is available from 9:30 a.m. to 4:15 p.m. This program includes instruction for first time skiers ages 4 to 12 and first time snowboarders ages 7 to 12, lunch, snacks, ski or snowboard equipment rental, and helmet rental.

Introduction to Skiing/Snowboarding Lesson Package

Sample the fun at Badger Pass with our Introductory Ski or Snowboard lesson package. These one- to two-hour first-time beginner ski lessons are offered at 10 a.m. and 2 p.m. daily.

The Next Step Lesson Package

Improve your new skills! Offered at 10 a.m. and 2 p.m., this one- to two-hour program is a perfect refresher course for the novice or intermediate skier. Take the morning course, practice what you learned, and come back for the afternoon course to fine tune your new skills. This package is for skiers ages 7 and up.

For those seeking individual attention or who want to hone a specific skill, private ski lessons offer one-on-one instruction. Private lesson times are at 9 a.m. and 1 p.m. Reservations are recommended.

Camping

Yosemite has 13 campgrounds within the park, each in a wonderful setting with sweeping views or forest canopies - and plenty of room to relax. Yosemite National Park camping offers a wide variety of options.

Yosemite Mountaineering School

The Yosemite Mountaineering School offers guided over-night back-country trips. With many pre-planned trips anyone can join, plus the option to plan a custom trip just for you and your group, we make it easy to experience the Park.

Canvas Tent Cabins

If you have camping sensibility but would look forward to something roomier and more comfortable, you'll enjoy tent cabins in Curry Village, Housekeeping Camp, Tuolumne Meadows, White Wolf and all the Yosemite High Camps. Visit our Lodging section for more information.

Traditional Tent Camping

Setting up your own tent, cooking over a fire ring or camp stove, sleeping under the stars. This type of camping is the ultimate National Park experience!

For more information on Campgrounds in Yosemite, visit the National Park Service site, or call (877) 444-6777.

Are you looking for some camping supplies? Stores in Yosemite Park carry camping essentials, including fuel for your cooking stoves. We usually stock the following fuel types here in the park:

  • Denatured alcohol
  • White gas
  • Coleman Propane canisters
  • Coleman PowerMax canisters
  • Isobutane canisters - both the threaded and puncture style.

RV Camping

No tents, no unpacking, just park, put up the canopy and start relaxing. Yosemite has 10 campgrounds that accommodate RVs and trailers. There are no RV hookups within the park, but there are dump stations in various locations. Visit the NPS site for more information on RV Camping.

Wilderness Camping

94% (1,101 square miles) of Yosemite is wilderness. See it by packing your backpack and finding a nice remote spot to sleep under the stars. You'll need a wilderness permit, information on being Bear Aware and a bear canister. For a wealth of information on Yosemite camping sites in the backcountry, see the NPS backpacking page.

Rock Climbing

The Yosemite Mountaineering School and Guide Service has been rock climbing in Yosemite since 1969. We know Yosemite better than anyone and love sharing our knowledge and skills with you.

Climbing Lessons at Yosemite

Offered daily April thru October, our Yosemite rock climbing classes are designed to give climbers of all levels new and extended skill sets. Reservations are strongly recommended, and customized classes are also available. Call (209) 372-8344 for more information about any of our Yosemite rock climbing lessons.

All of our climbing classes average seven hours in length, unless otherwise noted, so prepare for a full day of fun. Classes meet daily at 8:30 am at either the Mountaineering School in Curry Village or in Tuolumne Meadows. Each class builds upon the other - and they are designed to be taken sequentially, so that you can pick the single class you need, design the perfect multi-day seminar or come up every couple of weeks and complete our entire Yosemite climbing training program. Maximum size for private and group classes are 6 students per instructor.

Minimum age for climbing lessons is 12. Children that are 10 and 11 years old can join the regular classes with a supervising adult present. Children under 10 can participate in a private lesson with an accompanying adult.

Biking

With over 12 miles of paved bike trails in Yosemite National Park, you can explore Yosemite at your own pace, stopping to take photos and soak in the magnificent views at your leisure.

Bike Rentals

Rental bikes, trailers and helmets are available for all ages of riders. For the visually or physically impaired, we have a tandem bike and a hand crank bike.

DNC provides each rider an orientation regarding bike trails and use of a hand crank bike that will accommodate anyone with use of their upper body. All two seated bikes are reserved for visually impaired riders. Advanced reservations required for rental.

Planning Guide

Pick Your Perfect Day for a Trip to Yosemite

“Pick Your Perfect Day” Planning Guides provide ideas for enjoyable, fun-filled day-long experiences during warmer months in Yosemite without driving your personal vehicle. These guides suit a wide range of interests, ability levels, and ages. While these guides were originally designed for guests riding public transportation to Yosemite National Park, all park guests are encouraged to leave their cars parked and use these Yosemite trip planning guides to have their own perfect day in Yosemite. Check back often for new and exciting "Pick Your Perfect Day" Planning Guides. Better yet, join us on our Yosemite Facebook Page for updates and to share your ideas for a perfect Yosemite trip.

Tour and Trail Ride

Start the day with the wind in your hair, and then saddle up for a true western adventure! We’ll take you around Yosemite Valley for a breathtaking open-air Valley Floor Tour led by a Yosemite Park Ranger. After the tour and some lunch, you’ll saddle up for a guided trail ride on mule or horseback. During this perfect day, you’ll see the world-famous icons of Yosemite Valley and hear all sorts of stories about Yosemite’s natural and cultural history.

Bike and Hike

You can really soak it all in on your own terms with a day of biking and hiking in Yosemite Valley. If your Yosemite trip is all about big waterfalls, then this this day - and its hike to Vernal Fall - will put a smile on your face (even when other seasonal waterfalls are dry). Round out your perfect day discovering the stories of Yosemite’s natural and cultural history with visits to the Valley Visitor Center and Yosemite Museum.

Legacies and Luxuries

Spend the day tracing the footsteps of some of Yosemite’s legendary characters and discovering the luxuries hidden among the mountains and waterfalls of Yosemite Valley. See Yosemite in a new light on a guided photo walk and discover the beauty that has inspired photographers for years. Afterward, treat yourself to an afternoon of elegance and nostalgia with lunch and a tour at the historic Four-Diamond Ahwahnee hotel.

Fun With Kids

Treat your kids, and your own inner child to a perfect day that starts with a Children’s Theater performance filled with laughter and entertainment. Then fill your day with fun Junior Ranger activities that guide your exploration of the Valley using the free shuttle buses. At the end of the day, your child can be sworn in as an official Yosemite Junior Ranger in the Visitor’s Center (Adults can be Junior Rangers too. Don’t be shy.)

Family Fun

You and your young one will have a blast in Yosemite! The fun starts with an outdoor theatrical performance just for families. Then jump on some rental bikes to explore Yosemite Valley--you never know what you might find!
